Beyti Sarma

Ozan Atesmen

Turkey

Beyti is a Turkish dish consisting of minced meat or meat, grilled on a skewer, served with lavash, topped with tomato sauce and yogurt. The name of the dish comes from Beyti GÃ¼ler, owner of the popular Beyti restaurant in Istanbul.

30 Minutes

30 Minutes

Ingredients

Nutrition Facts

Cooking Tip

You can get more delicious meatballs by resting the meatballs in the refrigerator for 1 hour.

Cooking Tips

When shaping your meatballs, you should be careful not to be too thin. If it's too thin, your meatballs will be dry.

Cooking Tip

Applying butter on your phyllo will both increase its flavor and prevent it from drying out.

Step-By Step Recipe

1. Mix the onion, garlic, breadcrumbs, eggs, salt, pepper, and chili flakes through a mixer to form a mixture.

2. Put all the meatball ingredients in a bowl. Mix well until the ingredients are combined. After kneading, wrap it with cling film and rest in the refrigerator for 20 minutes. Take the ingredients for the tomato paste sauce in a saucepan and cook until it thickens.

3. Take enough meat for a medium-sized kebab from the rested meatballs. Shape it into an elongated shape, like a kebab, to be slightly thick.

4. Lay one of the rectangular phyllo on the counter and place one of the meatballs. Spread the tomato paste on the phyllo and then wrap the phyllo in the meatballs. Repeat the process until all the meatballs are finished.

5. Slice the wrapped meatballs to medium thickness. Transfer to a baking tray lined with baking paper and bake in a preheated oven at 190 degrees for 30 minutes in a controlled manner.

6. Arrange the cooked beyti wraps to form a circle towards the edge of the serving plate. Drizzle the tomato sauce over them.

7. Finally, put yogurt in the middle of the plate, sprinkle with parsley and serve the wraps. Bon Appetit!
